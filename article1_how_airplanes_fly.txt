HOW AIRPLANES FLY

Airplanes fly based on the principles of aerodynamics. The movement of
air around the aircraft generates four important forces that determine
whether the plane can stay in the air.

The four forces of flight are:

1.  Lift – the force that pushes the aircraft upward.
2.  Weight – the gravitational force pulling the aircraft downward.
3.  Thrust – the forward force produced by engines or propellers.
4.  Drag – the resistance caused by air moving around the aircraft.

For an airplane to fly, lift must be greater than weight, and thrust
must be greater than drag. Wings are shaped so that air moves faster
over the top than underneath. This difference in air pressure produces
lift.

Engines provide thrust, while the tail and control surfaces stabilize
and steer the aircraft.
