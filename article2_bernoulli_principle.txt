THE BERNOULLI PRINCIPLE AND AIRPLANE FLIGHT

One of the key principles used to explain airplane lift is the Bernoulli
principle. It states that when the speed of a fluid increases, its
pressure decreases.

Airplane wings are designed so that air flows faster across the curved
top surface than underneath the wing. Because the air above the wing
moves faster, the pressure there becomes lower than the pressure below
the wing.

This pressure difference produces an upward force known as lift. Lift
allows airplanes to rise off the ground and stay in the air during
flight.

Understanding the Bernoulli principle is essential for studying
aerodynamics and the physics of aviation.
