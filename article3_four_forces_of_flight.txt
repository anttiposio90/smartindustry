THE FOUR FORCES OF FLIGHT

Every airplane in flight is affected by four fundamental forces:

Lift – generated by the wings when air flows over them. Weight – caused
by gravity pulling the aircraft downward. Thrust – produced by engines
that push the aircraft forward. Drag – air resistance that slows the
aircraft down.

These forces act in opposition. Lift works against weight, while thrust
works against drag.

A stable flight occurs when lift balances weight and thrust balances
drag. Pilots control these forces using throttle, pitch, and aircraft
control surfaces.

Understanding these forces is essential for pilots, engineers, and
anyone studying aviation.
