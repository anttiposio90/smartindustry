AERODYNAMICS OF FLIGHT

Heavier-than-air flight depends on balancing several forces. For an
aircraft to fly steadily, lift must balance its weight and thrust must
overcome drag.

Wings generate lift by redirecting airflow downward and creating
pressure differences around the airfoil shape. Engines produce thrust
that pushes the aircraft forward through the air.

Drag is reduced by smooth aircraft design and efficient wing shapes.
Engineers carefully design aircraft structures and materials to keep
weight low while maintaining strength.

The balance of these forces allows aircraft to take off, cruise through
the air, and land safely.
