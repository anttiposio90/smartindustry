A SHORT HISTORY OF FLIGHT

The development of the airplane began with early experiments in
aerodynamics and gliders during the 18th and 19th centuries.

George Cayley is often considered the father of modern aeronautics. In
the late 1700s he proposed the concept of a fixed-wing aircraft with
separate systems for lift, propulsion, and control.

In the early 1900s the Wright brothers built and flew the first
successful powered airplane. Their major innovation was the ability to
control the aircraft in three axes: roll, pitch, and yaw.

Since then aviation has developed rapidly, evolving from simple
propeller aircraft to modern jet airliners capable of crossing oceans in
a few hours.
